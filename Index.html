Cybersecurity Quiz for Teens

68% of data breaches involve a human element. As well as data breaches, human error is often the root cause of cyberattacks such as stolen credentials, phishing attempts, ransomware, or wire fraud attacks — to name a few.

Posting Personal Information Online

Sharing personal details like your address, phone number, or school on social media can expose you to strangers who might misuse it. Be cautious about what you post publicly. Always use privacy settings to control who sees your information.

Phishing and Smishing Risks

Phishing (via email) and smishing (via text) are scams where attackers trick you into sharing personal info or clicking bad links. Never click links or share details from unknown sources. Always verify the sender before responding.

Checking and Ensuring Links and URLs

Fake links can lead to scams or malware. Always check a URL’s spelling and domain (like .com or .org) before clicking. Hover over links to see the real address and only trust official sources.
